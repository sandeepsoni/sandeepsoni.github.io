I'm a PhD student in Computer Science advised by Jacob Eisenstein at the Georgia Institute of Technology . I work on computational linguistics, focusing on measuring and modeling language change, computational social science and digital humanities. I'm expecting to graduate in Summer 2020.

Research

My research combines methods in machine learning, natural language processing and social networks to model language variation and change. A complete list of publications can be found here, on Google scholar. Here are some selected papers:

  • Follow the Leader: Documents on the Leading Edge of Semantic Change Get More Citations. Soni, Lerman, and Eisenstein. Preprint.
  • Detecting Social Influence in Event Cascades by Comparing Discriminative Rankers. Soni, Ramirez, and Eisenstein. CD Workshop at KDD 2019.
  • Calendar.help: Designing a Workflow-Based Scheduling Agent with Humans in the Loop. Cranshaw, Elwany, Newman, Kocielnik, Yu, Soni, Teevan, and Monroy-Hernandez. CHI 2017.
  • The Social Dynamics of Language Change in Online Networks. Goel, Soni, Goyal, Paparrizos, Wallach, Diaz, and Eisenstein. SocInfo 2016.
  • Modeling Factuality Judgments in Social Media Text. Soni, Mitra, Gilbert, and Eisenstein. ACL 2014.
